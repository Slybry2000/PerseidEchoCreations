§ I

The hard part of marketing is not stopping.

You know you should show up every week. Keeping it up — while you actually run the business — is the part nobody has time for.

Sound familiar?
  • You start strong, then go quiet for a month.
  • Writing and scheduling posts is a second job you never wanted.
  • When work gets busy, marketing is the first thing to drop.
  • You only market when things are slow — exactly backwards.

And quiet compounds the wrong way. Every week you don’t show up, the audience drifts and referrals cool — then the next push starts from a standstill. It’s rarely the best business that wins a market. It’s the one that never disappears.
