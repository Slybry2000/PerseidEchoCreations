In Development

Convergent Court

A two-agent decision court where Claude argues as architect and Codex as implementer — designed to refuse fluent-but-wrong answers instead of producing them.

See what's on the desk
01

Two agents, two roles, one debate.

Claude takes the architect seat, Codex takes the implementer seat. They have to converge on the framing of a question before either is allowed to answer it.

02

Schema-validated rounds of framing and rebuttal.

Each round has a structured contract. Off-topic, hand-wavy, or fluent-but-wrong responses fail validation instead of slipping through as confident output.

03

Refuse out loud when no honest answer exists.

If the agents can't converge on a framing, the court returns a visible refusal — not a smoothed-over guess. The disagreement is the signal.

04

Local-first, git as the audit trail.

A bash + Python orchestrator running Claude Code and the OpenAI Codex CLI on your machine. Every block is committed to git as a two-agent decision record — no chat UI, no judge, no smoothing.

About

From CX transformation to AI Solutions Practice.

Perseid Echo Creations is the studio I run as a solo operator. It now covers both the products I'm building and the AI agent work I do for other businesses.

Twenty-one years in customer experience and digital transformation. Now building products of my own — and the agents that run them.

My focus is on AI agentic systems — designing workflows where AI doesn't just assist, but actually carries work forward. Systems that:

  • generate ideas
  • critique and improve them
  • route decisions
  • and produce real, usable outputs

The products came first — The Green Felt Club, Bristlecone, Convergent Court. Each one needed agents to do real work behind the scenes, and that's where I learned the operating side: setting them up, keeping them running as models change, and stepping in when something breaks. That toolkit is what I now bring to other businesses.

The biggest shift hasn't been learning AI. It's been changing how I think about work:

Less doing everything myself.
More designing systems that do the work with me.

Transitioning openly. Building in public. Getting better every week.
